Shoyu Ahi Poke Recipe

Poke is a popular seafood dish in Hawaii. It is made of raw fish, sauce to marinate and different types of garnishes. Shoyu Ahi Poke is a traditional Hawaiian poke bowl that uses soy sauce marinate and yellow fin tuna. Poke means to "section" or to "slice or cut." The dish can be served with steamed rice.

Prep Time: 25 mins

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh sashimi-grade ahi or yellow fin tuna sliced in 1 inch cubes
  • 1 1/2 tbsp soy sauce plus more to taste
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 3/4 tsp Hawaiian salt ('alaea) plus more to taste
  • 1/4 cup thinly sliced Maui or yellow onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions (green parts only)
  • 1/8 tsp gochugaru (Korean red chili powder)
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped toasted macadamia nuts
  • 2 cups steamed rice for serving

Steps

  1. In a bowl, combine cubed ahi, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, Maui (or yellow) onion, green onions gochugaru, and toasted macadamia nuts and gently toss with your hands or spoon. Adjust seasoning to your liking
  2. Serve over steamed rice if desired and enjoy immediately
